# Ideas / product direction (from the user)

## North-star UX: "supermarket scanner"
The user goes **sticker by sticker**; the phone **quickly and automatically** shows
whether to put each one in the **KEEP** pile or the **DISCARD** pile (based on what
they still need). Fast, hands-free, glanceable — like scanning products at a checkout.
No tapping, repeats ignored within a session.

## Front camera by default (phone flat on the table)
- Phone lies flat, screen up. User shows the **back** of each sticker to the **front
  (selfie) camera**.
- Why it's clever: the user sees the **fronts** (the nice images) while the camera
  reads the **backs** (the codes — the easiest side to detect). No tripod needed.
- Default = front camera; keep a way to switch to the **back camera** (better quality,
  and easier to test on a tripod).
- The front-camera feed is mirrored — de-mirror (flip horizontally) before OCR.

## Neat trick (later): screen as a flashlight
With the front camera, the **screen brightness can illuminate the sticker back**,
making codes easier to read in low light. Ideally not needed, but a nice enhancement
(e.g. a bright frame around the preview while scanning).
